Generation Methodology
Persona-conditioned probabilistic sampling — 330 per-persona distributions extracted from 2,000 original rows, used as generation priors with Dirichlet noise.
Step 1 — Extract Per-Persona Distributions
330 distributions extracted (6 personas × 55 columns) from original 2K dataset.
Step 2 — Dirichlet Noise Injection
α = real_freq × 80 + 0.5 per categorical prior to simulate natural survey variance.
Step 3 — Persona-Conditioned Sampling
Each row assigned to a persona, all 55 columns sampled from that persona's distributions.
Step 4 — Missing Value Replication
style_secondary ~51.4% missing replicated exactly to match original patterns.
Step 5 — Validation
Binary column means within ±3.5%, persona proportions exact (±0%).

AUC 0.700 Acc 58.6%
Note on accuracy: Purchase intent is a 3-class problem (Interested / Maybe / Not Interested) with imbalanced classes (55% / 35% / 10%). ~58% accuracy is meaningful — it significantly outperforms the 55% naive baseline. AUC of 0.70 indicates good discriminative ability across all three classes.
